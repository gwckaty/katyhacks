What is Katy Youth Hacks?

Katy Youth Hacks is a 36-hour long hackathon hosted by Girls Who Code Katy for students from all around the world. Our theme for this year is social equity - we hope to guide participants to take a greater role in fighting social problems while encouraging underrepresented groups to engage in computer science and tech development. By challenging high schoolers to solve real-world problems with technology, KatyYouthHacks aims to inspire the next generation of developers.

FAQ

Who can participate?

This event is limited to high school (and middle school) students. Underrepresented minorities and students of low socioeconomic status are especially encouraged to participate. If you have graduated from high school, you can only apply to be a mentor.

How much does it cost to participate?

This event is 100% free!

What if I can't code?

No problem! We will be hosting many workshops to help new hackers learn valuable skills and create their own original projects! Additionally, several of the prize categories are judged on ideation rather than code.

How many people can be in a team?

Up to four people are allowed on one team.

I have another question...

If you have a question, do not hesitate to email us at contact@katyhacks.org. Feel free to also contact us on our Discord.
